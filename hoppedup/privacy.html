Privacy Policy for HoppedUp

Effective Date: July 14, 2026

This privacy policy applies specifically to the HoppedUp app developed by Rough Idea Software.

HoppedUp ("we," "our," or "the app") is anonymous by design. There are no accounts, no profiles, no usernames, and no social features. This policy explains what information the app handles, where it is stored, and the choices you have.

1. Summary

We do not operate any servers of our own, we do not collect analytics, and we never sell or share your data with third parties. The app handles two kinds of information: your personal check-in history, which stays on your device, and anonymous community contributions (a beer's name, your rating, and an optional short note), which are shared to a public database operated by Apple so that everyone can see how a brewery's beers are rated. Neither is ever linked to your name or identity.

2. Your Check-In History (Stays on Your Device)

The breweries you visit, the beers you rate, your ratings, and your notes are stored in a database on your device. This history is not synced to us and we cannot see it. Deleting the app deletes it.

3. Anonymous Community Ratings and Notes

When you rate a beer at a brewery that has a public map listing, the app shares the brewery's map identifier, the beer's name, your rating value, and — if you wrote one — a short note, to a shared public database (Apple CloudKit). This is what powers the community board: average ratings and rating counts for each beer.

These contributions are anonymous. No name, email, account, or profile exists in HoppedUp, and nothing shown in the app ever identifies who rated what. An anonymised Apple identifier is used internally for one purpose only: making sure each person counts once per beer (your newest rating replaces your older one). It is never displayed and never combined with anything identifying.

Your note is never displayed to other users. Notes exist solely to power the on-device AI summary described in section 4, and they are length-limited and filtered for offensive language before sharing. Please be aware that notes are stored in a shared public database: although the app never displays them, you should not write anything in a note that could identify you or anyone else.

4. On-Device AI Summaries

On devices that support Apple Intelligence, the app summarises a beer's community notes into a short impression (for example, "crisp and sessionable — a crowd favourite"). This summary is generated entirely on your device by Apple's on-device model. Note content is never sent to us or to any third party for summarisation, and summaries are always labelled as AI-generated.

5. Location

The app asks for while-using location access for one purpose: showing breweries near you. Searches are performed through Apple Maps under Apple's privacy policy. Your location is never stored by the app, never attached to any rating, and never transmitted to us. You can decline or revoke location access at any time in Settings; searching for breweries by name works without it.

6. Reporting and Moderation

Community summaries can be reported anonymously from within the app. Reports contain only the brewery and beer concerned — nothing about you. We review reports and may remove contributed content that breaks these rules.

7. Usage Data and Analytics

The app does not track or transmit any usage data, analytics, or personal activity, and it contains no advertising or tracking technology.

8. Third-Party Services

HoppedUp does not use any third-party services or analytics tools. The only external services involved are Apple's: CloudKit (the shared public database for community ratings), Apple Maps (brewery search), and Apple Intelligence (on-device summaries), each operating under Apple's terms and privacy policy. Community features require being signed in to iCloud.

9. Data Retention and Deletion

Your local history is removed when you delete the app. Community contributions are anonymous and each new rating you make for a beer replaces your previous one. If you would like your community contributions removed entirely, contact us with the brewery and beers concerned and we will remove them from the shared database.

10. Age

HoppedUp is an app about beer. It is intended for users of legal drinking age in their country and is not directed at children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children.

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected here, and the effective date above will be updated.

12. Contact
